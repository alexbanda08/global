Methodology and caveats

Data. Binance perpetual-futures USDT-margined OHLCV bars. BTC/ETH/SOL histories start 2019, altcoins start when they first listed on Binance (SUI 2023-05, TON 2022-10). Loaded from
the local features/multi_tf/ parquet store at 1h, 2h, and 4h.

Signal families tested (all long + short). (1) RangeKalman L+S -- mean-reverting Kalman-EMA with deviation bands, direction gated by a regime SMA. (2) BB-Break L+S -- Bollinger band
breakout + regime SMA gate. (3) Keltner + ADX L+S -- Keltner channel breakout gated by ADX strength + regime SMA. For each coin, the best config was chosen from a small per-family grid
(, lengths, multipliers, risk, TF).

Execution model. Signals fire at bar close; fills at next-bar open ▒ 3 bps slippage. Exits: TP at +tpūATR, SL at -slūATR, trailing stop at -trailūATR from high-water mark, forced exit at max-hold
bars. Position sizing: risk_per_trade ū equity „ (slūATR), capped at leverage_cap ū equity „ price. Taker fee of 0.045% charged on entry and exit notional.

Performance accounting. CAGR is compounded annual return on equity from a $10,000 start. "Net" CAGR subtracts a modeled 8% APR funding drag ū average leverage ū time-in-market.
Sharpe is annualized using the natural bar count per year at each TF. Drawdown is the maximum peak-to-trough decline of the equity curve. Profit factor is sum(winning P&L;) / |sum(losing
P&L;)|.

What this report IS. A per-coin optimization showing the best single-config for each asset, with a walk-forward out-of-sample check. For each coin the same locked config is re-simulated in two
slices: IS (2019-2023) and OOS (2024-2026). The "Walk-forward verdict" column flags a config as holding when OOS Sharpe  50% of IS Sharpe. TON has no IS history (first listing 2024-08)
and is shown OOS-only.

What this report is NOT. A go-live spec. Expect 20-40% haircut on CAGR from slippage, borrowing frictions, venue outages, and regime change. Paper trade each coin for 4+ weeks before
real capital; set a -45% circuit breaker per sub-account; re-audit every 6 months. LINK (37%) and INJ (29%) fall short of the 55% target -- use them for diversification, not as standalone
aggressive allocations.
