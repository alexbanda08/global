## 2. What we proved is wrong about the existing live system

### A. The 5-bp HYBRID reversal threshold is NOT the problem
The diagnosis assumed the threshold was too tight. Backtest holdout actually shows **3 bp performs best**:
- rev=3 bp / volume / 5m: HO 67.2% hit, +$184 PnL
- rev=5 bp: HO 63.2%, +$151
- rev=8 bp: HO 57.6%, +$93

Tighter is better, **as long as the exit on trigger is HEDGE_HOLD**.

### B. The HYBRID bid-exit fallback IS the problem
Live VPS3 V2 (HYBRID): hit 22-30%, total PnL -$2,820.
Live VPS2 V1 (HEDGE_HOLD): hit 44-49%, total PnL -$1,857.
Gap = ~20 pp hit / -$1k PnL. The `exited_at_bid` outcomes (when buy-opposite fails, sell at own bid) systematically lock in ~5% spread loss.

**Recommendation: kill the bid-exit fallback, period.** Use HEDGE_HOLD. If buy-opposite fails, hold to resolution. Don't sell at bid.

### C. Volume mode is structurally negative-EV at taker fills
Backtest volume @ HEDGE_HOLD: ~57% hit / +5% ROI.
Live VPS2 volume @ HEDGE_HOLD: 44-49% hit / negative ROI.
**~10 pp simulator-vs-live gap on volume.** Volume mode at taker fills loses live even when the simulator says it wins. Don't run volume.

The whole bull case lives on **sniper q10**, which:
- Has 78-86% hit rate in forward-walk holdout (vs volume's 53-57%).
- Has never fired live (VPS3 cold-start). **Live edge is unproven.**
