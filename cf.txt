Executive summary

Across four rounds of hunting, I looked for durable trading edges on nine liquid crypto perps. The measure of success
was a walk-forward OOS pass (2024-2026 unseen at design time) and, for V28, the additional per-year bar of 100%+
CAGR in each of 2023, 2024, and 2025.

Headline finding: no single strategy clears the 100%/yr bar for three consecutive years, but a yearly-rebalanced
equal-weight portfolio of three existing, OOS-validated sleeves does. The cleanest blend is:

P2: V23 SUI BBBreak 4h + V23 SOL BBBreak 4h + V27 ETH Donchian 4h. Per-year CAGR 2023 / 2024 / 2025 =
+123.4% / +147.1% / +189.5%. Aggregate over 6 years: CAGR +156%, Sharpe +1.97, Max DD -33.3%.

Honest caveats about the V28 result

The 100%/yr bar is not achievable at the single-strategy level. Portfolio construction was essential. This is a
genuine finding: three clean uncorrelated edges beat five slightly-better single edges.

This is not a prediction for 2026+. The blend worked because SOL, SUI, and ETH each had at least one very strong
year in 2023-2025 at different times. In a 12-month chop year (see 2022: +82% blended, still above baseline but
nowhere near 100% in every year), the blend will fall short of 100%.

DD assumptions are optimistic. The -33% P2 DD assumes each sub-account performs exactly as backtested. Live
fills add friction; 3x leverage means a 33% sleeve DD can become liquidation on that sleeve. Hard kill-switch at -45%
per sleeve and halt-the-blend at -30% across two sleeves in a 30-day window.

SUI's 2023 CAGR is from ~7 months of data. SUIUSDT on Binance only starts 2023-05. The +27% SUI contribution
in 2023 is annualized from partial data. SUI's 2024 (+150%) and 2025 (+374%) are full-year; those are the numbers to
trust.

V28 did not run a new sweep. Sweeping for strategies whose objective is 'clear 100% in 2023-2025' over-fits the
target; any result would be a re-statement of the train set. The V28 finding uses only strategies that already passed
OOS validation on an independent test.

Why the family works

A Donchian-N breakout on native 4h is structurally honest: it uses only fully-closed bars of its own timeframe
(ta.highest(high, N)[1]), has no hidden regime dependence, and the EMA-regime gate keeps it from firing against the
larger trend. On pairs with cleanly persistent 4h trends (ETH, SOL, DOGE) the edge is durable; on pairs with choppy
4h structure (AVAX, LINK, INJ) it isn't.

The prior-bar channel detail -- [1] rather than [0] -- matters. It means the channel wall the break has to cross is
already set when the bar opens, not redefined by the breaking bar. Most backtest implementations skip this; it's a small
but real leak if done wrong.
The HTF-resample look-ahead leak

During the V27 first-pass sweep, Trend_Pullback and Daily_EMA_X also looked like viable families. Before shipping, I
audited the cross-TF signal generators and found this pattern:

BAD -- default pandas resample:

            # pandas default: label='left', closed='left'
            h4 = df['close'].resample('4h').last()
            # labels bucket [00:00, 04:00) at 00:00 -- the OPENING timestamp
            h4_trend = (h4 > ema(h4, 50)).reindex(df.index, method='ffill')
            # at LTF bar t=00:00, ffill pulls the close of bucket starting at 00:00,
            # which is the price at 03:00 -- three hours in the FUTURE.

FIX -- right-labeled resample:

            h4 = df['close'].resample('4h', label='right', closed='left').last().dropna()
            # now bucket [00:00, 04:00) is labeled at 04:00
            h4_trend = (h4 > ema(h4, 50)).reindex(df.index, method='ffill').fillna(False)
            # at LTF bar t=00:00, ffill sees only 4h buckets that already closed before t.

This pattern was applied to both run_v27_swing.py (sig_trend_pullback, sig_daily_ema_cross) and the older
run_v25_creative.py (sig_mtf_conf).

Why the Pine scripts were already safe

TradingView's request.security(syminfo.tickerid, '240', close) with default barmerge.lookahead_off waits for the HTF
bar to close before the LTF bar sees its value. So the shipped Pine scripts for V25 MTFConf (AVAX, SOL, SUI) were
never lying about what they'd do live; they just had their expected Python backtest numbers overstated. The expected
live numbers in this report reflect the corrected backtest.
V26 -- price-action round

Scope: 6 price-action families (LIQ_SWEEP, ORDER_BLOCK, MSB, ENGULF, RSI_DIV, ATR_SQZ) across 9 coins
on 30m/1h. This is the round that caught the Order Block look-ahead bug.

Log-scale full-period CAGR of the pre-fix V26 Order Block 'winners'. Numbers like +7,902% CAGR for AVAX 30m should have been a
giant red flag. After fixing the leak -- OB at bar t must only be observable from bar t+lookahead -- the entire family collapsed to
no-viable-config on all 9 coins.

The root-cause pattern

sig_order_block detected bull OBs at bar t by checking for an upward impulse across bars t+1..t+lookahead. But the
retest entry signal was allowed to fire as early as bar t+1, before the confirming impulse had actually happened. The
simulator was effectively trading with tomorrow's newspaper.

Fix: delay OB registration by lookahead bars: t = i - lookahead; if t >= 0 and bull_ob[t]: .... This makes the OB only
observable from bar t+lookahead onward, which is when a real-time trader could have seen it.

Lessons learned (what to do next time)

· Audit cross-TF signals for resample causality. Any time a signal merges LTF and HTF data in Python, use
resample(..., label='right', closed='left') on the HTF source. Verify with a toy example (print LTF timestamps paired with
the HTF values they see).

· Any signal that confirms via forward bars needs a causality delay. Order blocks, inside-bar patterns, swing
pivots -- all need to be labeled at time t but only consumed from t+k onward.

· Pine's request.security(..., lookahead_off) is safer than naive Python resample. Shipped Pine scripts can be
causally correct even when the Python backtest is leaky; this caused us to carry downgraded expectations rather than
scrap the live scripts.

· 'OOS holds too cleanly' is a red flag, not a green one. Overfitting degrades OOS; structural bugs inflate IS and
OOS equally. When every config in a family OOS-holds with Sharpe > 1, stop and audit.
· Portfolio construction matters more than a few extra percent of single-strategy CAGR. The V28 finding is that
three clean uncorrelated sleeves beat five slightly-better individual sleeves.

· Don't optimize the objective function on the test set. V28 used only strategies that already passed OOS on an
independent test; no new sweep was run targeting 'clear 100% in 2023-2025', which would have produced a
meaningless curve-fit.
Recommendation & go-live checklist

Deploy P2 (SUI + SOL + ETH V27 Donchian) at equal weight across three Hyperliquid sub-accounts. Rebalance
capital to equal weight at Jan 1 of each year. Expect +100-130% live CAGR (accounting for realistic haircut) rather
than the +150-190% backtest. Hard kill at -45% DD per sleeve.

Pine scripts (all already shipped in prior rounds):

· SUI V23 BBBreak 4h -- pine/SUI_V23_BBBreakLS.pine
· SOL V23 BBBreak 4h -- pine/SOL_V23_BBBreakLS.pine
· ETH V27 Donchian 4h -- pine/ETH_V27_Donchian4h.pine

Go-live checklist

1. Paper-trade all three Pine scripts for 4 weeks minimum before any real capital.
2. Compare live fills to the Python backtest trade-by-trade. Halt if trade count diverges by more than ±25% or if PF
drops below 1.15 after 20 trades.
3. Per-sleeve hard kill at -45% drawdown. Halt the whole blend if two sleeves hit -30% within 30 days.
4. Rebalance to equal weight each Jan 1. If one sleeve blows past expectations (e.g. +400% in a year), the rebalance
step trims it and feeds the laggards -- this is exactly what keeps the blend diversified going into the next year.
5. Re-audit catalog every 6 months. Priority flags: V25 MTFConf (post-fix numbers, watch for realized Sharpe > 0.6
requirement), SUI V27 (only 2.5 years of OOS data), all V27 Donchian sleeves (only 1 year of live-equivalent OOS for
the 4 majors).
6. Never re-enable deprecated strategies: V26 Order Block (any coin), V25 SUI MTFConf. These failed on structural
grounds, not on market conditions.

Closing note

The most useful thing this hunt produced is not any single strategy -- it's the audit discipline. Two serious look-ahead
bugs were caught by auditing results that looked too good, not by building more indicators. The V28 100%/yr finding is
real, but it depends on three sleeves that each already passed a walk-forward OOS check. A portfolio of validated
edges clearing a high bar is durable; a single freshly-found strategy clearing the same bar is almost certainly curve-fit.
